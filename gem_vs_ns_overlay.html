Read the SHAPE, not coincidence: the two measure different things (table = pure transmit; NS = how much energy actually arrives, which folds in how often the ocean sends from each bearing). What matters — does the table stay near-full transmit across 50–90° where NS delivers strongly, and does it roll off by ~120–135° where NS decays to zero? A table that transmits hard past 135°, or below 40°, is over-open vs what reaches the node. Note gemLookup folds direction at 180°, so eg 95° and 85° share a row — asymmetry the bay may actually have is not representable.
